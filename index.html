The Watergate Scandal

The Political Crisis That Changed America Forever

What Was Watergate?

The Watergate scandal was a political crisis that began with a break-in at the Democratic National Committee headquarters in 1972 and ended with President Richard Nixon’s resignation. It exposed corruption, abuse of power, and illegal actions by government officials.

Timeline

June 17, 1972 — Break-in at Watergate

October 1973 — Saturday Night Massacre

July 24, 1974 — Supreme Court orders tape release

August 9, 1974 — Nixon resigns

Key Figures

Richard Nixon — 37th President of the United States

Bob Woodward & Carl Bernstein — Investigative journalists

John Dean — White House Counsel and key witness

Legacy

Watergate changed American politics by strengthening government oversight, increasing press freedom, and reinforcing the idea that no one is above the law.
