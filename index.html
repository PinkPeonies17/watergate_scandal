The Watergate Scandal

The Political Crisis That Changed America Forever

What Was Watergate?

The Watergate scandal was a political crisis in the early 1970s that began with a break‑in at the Democratic National Committee headquarters and led to President Richard Nixon’s resignation. It revealed abuses of power, obstruction of justice, and cover‑up attempts at the highest levels of government.

Timeline of Key Events

June 17, 1972 — Watergate break‑in.

Oct. 1973 — "Saturday Night Massacre."

July 24, 1974 — Supreme Court orders release of tapes.

Aug. 9, 1974 — Nixon resigns.

Key Figures

Richard Nixon — President who resigned due to Watergate cover‑up involvement.

Bob Woodward & Carl Bernstein — Journalists whose reporting exposed the scandal.

John Dean — White House Counsel who testified about the cover‑up.

Investigation & Legal Impact

Congressional hearings, the Supreme Court ruling in United States v. Nixon, and media investigation uncovered the White House’s role in the break‑in and cover‑up, leading to Nixon’s resignation and reforms in campaign finance and executive oversight.
